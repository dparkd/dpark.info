I am an interface artist.

The industry prefers the term product designer, but that title carries an expectation to design products and experiences that optimize for hyper-growth. This model of creation punishes creativity and exploration.

As a result, I frame all of my past work as artist residencies where I explore a particular space without the expectation of traditional product design requirements. If you’d like to invite me to your space as an artist-in-residence, email me at email@dpark.info
